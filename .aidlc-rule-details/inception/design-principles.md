# Inception Phase Design Principles Rules

1. **Understand User Needs**: Clearly identify the requirements and expectations of stakeholders early in the project.
2. **Iterative Development**: Emphasize feedback and make adjustments through repeatable cycles during the inception phase.
3. **Prioritize Features**: Focus on delivering the most critical features and requirements that provide value to users.
4. **Cross-Disciplinary Collaboration**: Encourage collaborative efforts among various disciplines to achieve a well-rounded understanding of the project's goals.
5. **Risk Management**: Identify and analyze potential risks early, applying appropriate mitigation strategies.
6. **Define Success Metrics**: Establish measurable success criteria that reflect project goals and user satisfaction.
7. **Embrace Change**: Stay flexible to changes based on insights gained throughout the inception phase.
8. **Document Everything**: Keep thorough documentation to capture decisions, designs, and rationale for future reference.
9. **Set Clear Goals**: Ensure all team members understand the overall objectives and their specific roles in achieving them.
10. **Stay User-Centric**: Maintain a constant focus on delivering value to users, adapting as their needs evolve throughout the project.
